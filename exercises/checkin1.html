How to Practice Meditation as a College Student

With the committments and obligations that come with being a college student, getting into meditation might be the last thing on your mind. You might be wondering, 'Why on Earth would I sit down and focus on my breathing when I'm juggling so many things?' But don't underestimate the power of an intentional breath. As simple as meditation seems, it packs a plethora of mental, emotional and physical health benefits that deserve to be prioritized (if you're not convinced, check out this article about the benefits of meditation).

Follow this short guide about how you can get started with meditation as a busy college student.

Getting Started

  1. Start small. Like with any new habit, starting small is usually the best approach. Whether with a guided meditation or on your own, meditate for small chunks of time, say 5-10 minutes each day, or whatever works for your schedule. I recommend this short guided meditation for getting started.
  2. Decide time of day. Decide whether you'd prefer meditating in the morning or at night.
  3. Be patient with yourself. It's okay if you skip a day or a week. Be compassionate with yourself. After all, that's what meditation is about.

Bonus Tips

  • Try out different setups before committing to one. I meditated at different times of the day and listened to different guided meditations before finding that 5-10 minutes before bed works best for me. Experiment to find what works for you.
  • Keep a habit tracker. I found that tracking how often I meditate motivates me to stay consistent, plus it shows me the progress I've made. Find what motivates you to continue meditating.
